# AS/NZS 1158.3.1 — Pedestrian Area (Category P) Lighting Requirements

Source: AS/NZS 1158 Australian Standard for Lighting of Roads and Public Spaces

## Overview

Category P lighting applies to pedestrian areas including parks, pathways, local roads, shared zones, and public spaces. The primary performance measure is horizontal illuminance (lux at ground level).

## P-Category Requirements Table

### P1 — Major Pedestrian Activity Areas
- Typical application: CBD plazas, transit hubs, major public gathering spaces
- Average horizontal illuminance: 14 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 7.0 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50
- Vertical illuminance (semi-cylindrical at 1.5m): 4 lux minimum (for facial recognition)

### P2 — High-Activity Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Shopping streets, busy park pathways, outdoor dining precincts
- Average horizontal illuminance: 10 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 5.0 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50

### P3 — Moderate Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Suburban commercial areas, busy parks, shared-use paths
- Average horizontal illuminance: 7 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 3.5 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50

### P4 — Moderate-Low Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Residential collector streets
- Average horizontal illuminance: 5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 2.5 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50

### P5 — Low Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Local residential streets
- Average horizontal illuminance: 3.5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 1.75 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50

### P6 — Low-Activity Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Quiet residential areas
- Average horizontal illuminance: 3.5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 0.75 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.21

### P7 — Minor Pedestrian Areas
- Typical application: Low-density residential
- Average horizontal illuminance: 1.5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 0.75 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50

### P8 — Minor Pedestrian Areas (Low Risk)
- Typical application: Low density, low risk residential
- Average horizontal illuminance: 1.5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 0.38 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.25

### P9 — Park Paths (Moderate Use)
- Typical application: Park pathways with moderate evening pedestrian activity
- Average horizontal illuminance: 2.0 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 1.0 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50
- Typical pole height: 3.5 to 5.0 metres
- Typical spacing: 15 to 25 metres

### P10 — Park Paths (Low Use)
- Typical application: Park pathways with low evening pedestrian activity
- Average horizontal illuminance: 1.0 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 0.50 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.50
- Typical pole height: 3.5 to 5.0 metres
- Typical spacing: 20 to 35 metres

### P11 — Outdoor Car Parks (Commercial)
- Average horizontal illuminance: 7 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 1.75 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.25

### P12 — Outdoor Car Parks (Residential/Low Use)
- Average horizontal illuminance: 3.5 lux
- Minimum horizontal illuminance: 0.88 lux
- Uniformity ratio (Emin/Eavg): 0.25

## General Design Rules for Category P

- Pole spacing is typically 3 to 5 times the mounting height. For example, a 4-metre pole on a park path gives 12 to 20 metre spacing.
- Minimum Colour Rendering Index (CRI/Ra): 60 for all categories. Higher categories (P1-P3) may require CRI >= 70.
- Recommended colour temperature for Melbourne: 3000K (warm white) to minimise ecological impact on wildlife and comply with City of Melbourne Urban Forest Strategy.
- Maintenance factor for LED luminaires: 0.85 to 0.90 (vs 0.70 for older HPS technology).
- Vertical illuminance (semi-cylindrical) is specified for higher P-categories (P1-P3) to ensure facial recognition capability, important for CCTV effectiveness and pedestrian safety.

## Category Selection Guidance

The selection of the appropriate P-category depends on:
1. Pedestrian activity level (volume of foot traffic, especially during evening hours)
2. Risk of crime or antisocial behaviour in the area
3. Proximity to high-activity zones (transit stops, commercial areas, schools)
4. Council design brief requirements (City of Melbourne often specifies P3 or higher for major park paths)

For Melbourne parks specifically:
- High-use paths (Fitzroy Gardens, Royal Park shared paths, Princes Park): P2 or P3
- Moderate-use paths: P9
- Low-use paths: P10
- Major park entrances and gathering areas: P1 or P2

## Adaptive Dimming Provisions

AS/NZS 1158.3.1 acknowledges that lighting may be reduced during periods of low pedestrian activity, provided the reduced level still meets the applicable lower P-category. For example:
- A path designed to P3 (7 lux) during peak hours may be dimmed to P5 (3.5 lux) after midnight if pedestrian volumes warrant it.
- A path designed to P2 (10 lux) may dim to P3 (7 lux) during low-activity periods.
This provision supports energy-efficient smart lighting systems.
