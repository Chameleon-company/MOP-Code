# AS/NZS 1158.4 — Pedestrian Crossing and School Zone Lighting

Source: AS/NZS 1158 Australian Standard, Part 4

## Pedestrian Crossing Lighting Requirements

AS/NZS 1158.4 specifies enhanced lighting at pedestrian crossings to ensure drivers can see pedestrians approaching or using the crossing.

### Key Requirements
- Minimum 50 lux vertical illuminance on the pedestrian, measured at 1.5 metres height, on the approach side visible to drivers
- Enhanced horizontal illuminance across the crossing area — typically at least double the adjacent road section lighting
- Asymmetric luminaires or supplementary poles are commonly used to achieve the required contrast to silhouette pedestrians against the road surface
- Lighting must be provided on both approach sides of the crossing for bidirectional roads

### Intersection Lighting
At signalised intersections, the area within 20 metres of the stop line on each approach should be lit to the V-category of the highest-class road entering the intersection, with a one-step category uplift:
- V5 road at intersection → V4 lighting required
- V4 road at intersection → V3 lighting required
- V3 road at intersection → V2 lighting required

### School Zone Crossings
School zone crossings follow the same AS/NZS 1158.4 requirements as standard pedestrian crossings. VicRoads/Department of Transport Victoria guidelines may add additional requirements:
- Flashing warning lights and signage
- Reduced speed zone (40 km/h) during school hours
- Enhanced visibility requirements for young pedestrians (shorter stature means lower detection height)

### Design Considerations for Crossings
- Pole placement should not obstruct sight lines between drivers and pedestrians
- Luminaires should be positioned to illuminate pedestrians from the driver's approach side
- Minimum colour rendering index of 60 (CRI >= 70 preferred for crossings)
- 3000K colour temperature recommended for Melbourne to maintain ecological compliance while ensuring adequate colour rendering
- Crossing lighting should operate at full intensity whenever the crossing is active (no dimming at crossings)

## Residential Street Lighting

### Category Selection for Residential Streets
- P5 (3.5 lux): Local residential streets with moderate pedestrian activity
- P6 (3.5 lux, lower uniformity 0.21): Low-activity residential areas
- P7 (1.5 lux): Minor residential streets, low-density suburbs
- P8 (1.5 lux, lower uniformity 0.25): Very low activity residential areas

### Residential Design Rules
- Pole height: typically 5-8 metres for residential streets
- Spacing: 4-5 times mounting height (e.g., 6m pole = 24-30m spacing)
- Single-side arrangement is common for narrow residential streets
- Staggered arrangement for wider streets (>8m carriageway)
- LED luminaires of 30-60W are typical for residential P5-P8 categories
