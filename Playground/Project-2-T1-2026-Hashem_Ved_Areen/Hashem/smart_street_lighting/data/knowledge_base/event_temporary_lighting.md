# Event and Temporary Lighting Guidelines

Source: Compiled from industry practice, AS/NZS 1158, and Melbourne event guidelines.

## Temporary Event Lighting Requirements

### Overview
AS/NZS 1158 does not specifically address temporary event lighting. However, the standard's permanent public space categories provide the baseline for temporary installations. Event lighting must meet or exceed the P-category requirements for the intended activity level.

### Minimum Requirements for Outdoor Events
- Ground-level illuminance: minimum 50 lux for high-density crowd areas
- Emergency egress paths: minimum 10 lux (P2 level) along all exit routes
- Stage/performance areas: 100-300 lux depending on filming requirements
- Food preparation areas: minimum 200 lux per food safety standards
- Vertical illuminance for CCTV: minimum 4 lux at 1.5m height in security-monitored zones

### Event Lighting Categories by Size

#### Small Events (up to 500 people)
- General area: P2-P3 category (7-10 lux average)
- Entry/exit points: P1 category (14 lux)
- Portable LED towers: 4-6 units for a 50m x 50m area
- Power requirement: approximately 2-4 kW total

#### Medium Events (500-3,000 people)
- General area: P1-P2 category (10-14 lux) minimum
- Entry/exit points: 20+ lux with directional signage lighting
- Portable LED towers: 10-20 units
- Power requirement: approximately 5-15 kW total
- Backup generator recommended

#### Large Events (3,000+ people)
- General area: 30-50 lux minimum for crowd safety
- Entry/exit points: 50+ lux
- Emergency lighting system: independent battery backup
- Portable LED towers: 20-40+ units
- Professional lighting design required
- Power requirement: 15-50+ kW
- Backup generator mandatory

### Equipment Options

#### Portable LED Tower Lights
- Typical output: 20,000-50,000 lumens per tower
- Power: 150-400W per tower
- Height: 3-7m telescopic mast
- Coverage: 15-25m radius per tower at ground level
- Fuel/power: mains-connected, generator, or battery/solar hybrid
- Cost: $200-500 per unit per event (hire)

#### Festoon String Lights
- Decorative/ambient lighting for low-activity areas
- Typical: 10-15W per metre, 2-5 lux at ground level
- Not suitable as primary safety lighting
- Good for pathways between activity zones

#### Temporary Pole-Mounted Luminaires
- Fixed to temporary poles or existing infrastructure
- Similar performance to permanent luminaires
- Installation time: 2-4 hours per unit
- Suitable for multi-day events or seasonal installations

### Event Lighting Design Process

1. **Assess the site:** Map the event area, identify entry/exit points, stages, food areas, and crowd flow paths
2. **Determine crowd density:** Expected peak attendance per zone
3. **Set illuminance targets:** Based on event size category and zone function
4. **Select equipment:** Portable towers for large areas, string lights for ambiance, pole-mounted for precision
5. **Plan power supply:** Calculate total wattage, plan cable routes, arrange generators if needed
6. **Design for egress:** Ensure all emergency exits maintain P2 minimum (10 lux) even if main power fails
7. **Consider neighbours:** Avoid light spill into residential areas; use shielded luminaires

### Melbourne-Specific Event Considerations

#### Federation Square
- Existing permanent lighting infrastructure (P1 level)
- Supplementary event lighting typically needed for large events
- Power supply available at multiple points
- Heritage and architectural lighting must be preserved

#### Birrarung Marr
- Riverside location with ecological sensitivity
- 3000K maximum colour temperature near river
- Temporary lighting must not spill onto water surface (impacts aquatic wildlife)
- Popular for events from 6pm to midnight

#### Carlton Gardens
- UNESCO World Heritage site (Royal Exhibition Building)
- Heritage lighting design constraints
- Temporary installations must not damage historic fabric
- Light levels must complement, not overwhelm, heritage lighting

#### Parks General
- All temporary lighting in City of Melbourne parks must use 3000K warm white
- Shielded luminaires required (ULOR = 0%)
- Equipment must be removed within 24 hours of event conclusion
- Noise restrictions on generators after 10pm in residential-adjacent parks

### Energy and Cost Estimates for Events

| Event Size | Equipment | Power Draw | Duration | Energy Cost |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Small (500 ppl) | 6 LED towers | 1.5 kW | 6 hours | ~$2 |
| Medium (2,000 ppl) | 15 LED towers | 4.5 kW | 6 hours | ~$6 |
| Large (5,000 ppl) | 30 LED towers | 12 kW | 6 hours | ~$15 |

Note: Energy cost is negligible for events. The major costs are equipment hire ($200-500/tower), labour for setup/pack-down ($500-2,000), and generator hire if needed ($300-1,000/day).
