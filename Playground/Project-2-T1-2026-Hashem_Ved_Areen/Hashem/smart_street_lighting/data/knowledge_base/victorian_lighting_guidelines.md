# Victorian Lighting Guidelines Beyond AS/NZS 1158

## VicRoads Supplement to AS/NZS 1158

VicRoads (now Department of Transport and Planning) publishes a supplement to AS/NZS 1158 with Victoria-specific requirements:

### Key Victorian Additions
- **Ecological sensitivity**: Parks within 500m of waterways or native habitat must use 3000K CCT maximum (warm white) to reduce impact on wildlife, particularly nocturnal species and migratory birds
- **Heritage areas**: Lighting in heritage-listed parks and gardens must use luminaires consistent with the heritage character. Fitzroy Gardens, Carlton Gardens, and Treasury Gardens have specific design guidelines
- **Shared paths**: Paths shared between cyclists and pedestrians require minimum P3 category regardless of traffic volume, due to collision risk
- **Playground proximity**: Areas within 50m of playground equipment require minimum P5 category during operating hours

## City of Melbourne Public Lighting Strategy

### Smart City Lighting Principles
1. **Connected infrastructure**: All new luminaires should be smart-city ready (DALI dimmable, sensor compatible)
2. **Adaptive dimming**: Default to traffic-responsive dimming schedules, with override capability for events
3. **Environmental lighting**: Minimise upward light ratio to < 1% (dark sky compliant where possible)
4. **Accessibility**: Ensure lighting supports wayfinding for people with disabilities (consistent colour temperature, minimum 70 CRI)

### Melbourne-Specific Standards
- All new public lighting must be LED (no new HPS installations since 2020)
- Minimum CRI of 70 for all pedestrian areas
- Maximum pole height of 6m in residential and park areas
- Recommended mounting height: 4m for park paths, 5m for shared paths, 6m for activity areas
- Luminaire cut-off angle: full cut-off (FCO) required to minimise light spill

## Parks Victoria Requirements

### Trail and Pathway Lighting
- All trailhead car parks: minimum P11 category
- Sealed paths: minimum P10 (low use) or P9 (moderate use)
- Unsealed paths: lighting not typically provided; solar bollards acceptable for way-marking
- Shared-use paths (cycling + walking): minimum P3

### Environmental Considerations
- Impact assessment required for new lighting within 200m of significant fauna habitat
- Amber LEDs (590nm) preferred near sensitive waterways (lowest insect attraction)
- Timer or motion-sensor activation preferred over dusk-to-dawn in ecologically sensitive areas
- Light spill onto waterways must not exceed 0.5 lux

## Transport for Victoria (TfV) Requirements

### Pedestrian Crossings
- All signalised pedestrian crossings: P1 category minimum
- Zebra crossings: P2 category minimum
- School crossings: P2 during operational hours, may dim to P5 outside hours

### Public Transport Stops
- Tram stops: P3 category minimum within 15m radius
- Bus stops: P5 category minimum
- Railway station forecourts: P2 category minimum

## Sustainability Victoria Guidelines

### Street Lighting Carbon Reduction Targets
- 50% reduction in public lighting energy by 2030 (from 2015 baseline)
- All public lighting to be LED by 2025 (largely achieved in Melbourne)
- Adaptive dimming should reduce baseline energy by minimum 25%
- Solar-powered lighting preferred for new installations in parks where grid connection cost exceeds $10,000

### Circular Economy Considerations
- Luminaire design should allow component-level replacement (driver, LED module)
- Minimum 15-year warranty required for public lighting luminaires
- End-of-life recycling plan required for all lighting components
