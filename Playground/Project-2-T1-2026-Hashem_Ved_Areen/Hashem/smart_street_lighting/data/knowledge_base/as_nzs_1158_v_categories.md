# AS/NZS 1158.1.1 — Vehicular Traffic (Category V) Lighting Requirements

Source: AS/NZS 1158 Australian Standard for Lighting of Roads and Public Spaces

## Overview

Category V lighting applies to roads where the primary concern is the driver's ability to see the road surface and obstacles. The performance measure is road surface luminance (candela per square metre, cd/m²).

## V-Category Requirements Table

### V1 — Freeways, Motorways, High-Speed Arterials
- Average luminance: 2.0 cd/m²
- Overall uniformity (Uo) minimum: 0.40
- Longitudinal uniformity (Ul) minimum: 0.70
- Threshold increment (TI) maximum: 10%

### V2 — Major Urban Arterials, Divided Roads
- Average luminance: 1.5 cd/m²
- Overall uniformity (Uo) minimum: 0.40
- Longitudinal uniformity (Ul) minimum: 0.70
- Threshold increment (TI) maximum: 10%

### V3 — Arterial Roads, Significant Urban Roads
- Average luminance: 1.0 cd/m²
- Overall uniformity (Uo) minimum: 0.40
- Longitudinal uniformity (Ul) minimum: 0.50
- Threshold increment (TI) maximum: 15%

### V4 — Sub-Arterial Roads, Collector Roads
- Average luminance: 0.75 cd/m²
- Overall uniformity (Uo) minimum: 0.40
- Longitudinal uniformity (Ul) minimum: 0.50
- Threshold increment (TI) maximum: 15%

### V5 — Lower-Order Collector Roads
- Average luminance: 0.50 cd/m²
- Overall uniformity (Uo) minimum: 0.35
- Longitudinal uniformity (Ul) minimum: 0.40
- Threshold increment (TI) maximum: 20%

## General Design Rules for Category V

- Pole spacing is typically 3 to 4 times the mounting height. For a 10-metre mounting height, this gives 30 to 40 metre spacing.
- Luminaire arrangements: single-side, staggered, or central-median configurations are chosen based on road width and target luminance.
- Maintenance factor for LED: 0.70 to 0.80, applied to initial design values.

## Intersection Lighting (AS/NZS 1158.4)

At signalised intersections, the area within 20 metres of the stop line on each approach should be lit to the V-category of the highest-class road entering the intersection, with a one-step category uplift. For example, if the road is V3, the intersection should achieve V2 levels.

### Pedestrian Crossing Requirements (AS/NZS 1158.4)
- Minimum 50 lux vertical illuminance on the pedestrian, measured at 1.5 metres height, on the approach side visible to drivers.
- Enhanced horizontal illuminance across the crossing area — typically at least double the adjacent road section lighting.
- Asymmetric luminaires or supplementary poles are commonly used to achieve the required contrast to silhouette pedestrians against the road surface.
- School zone crossings follow the same 1158.4 requirements. VicRoads/Department of Transport guidelines may add requirements for flashing lights and signage, but defer to 1158.4 for luminaire performance.
