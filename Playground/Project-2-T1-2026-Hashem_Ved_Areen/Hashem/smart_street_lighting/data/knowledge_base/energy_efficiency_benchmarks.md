# Energy Efficiency Guidelines and LED Benchmarks for Street Lighting

Source: Compiled from Australian energy efficiency publications, Victorian government data, BOM solar records, and municipal upgrade program reports.

## LED vs HPS (High Pressure Sodium) Comparison

### Minor Road / Category P Lighting
- HPS system: 150W lamp + 25W ballast = 175W total system draw
- LED replacement: 60-80W including driver
- Energy saving: 54-63% reduction

### Major Road / Category V Lighting
- HPS system: 250-400W lamp + 30-50W ballast = 280-450W total system draw
- LED replacement: 120-180W including driver
- Energy saving: 57-60% reduction

### Performance Comparison
- HPS system efficacy: 60-80 lumens per watt at system level
- LED system efficacy: 130-180 lumens per watt (current generation, 2025-2026)
- HPS colour temperature: approximately 2000K (amber/orange)
- LED colour temperature: 3000-4000K (warm to neutral white); 3000K preferred in Melbourne
- HPS Colour Rendering Index (CRI): 20-25
- LED Colour Rendering Index (CRI): 70-80+
- HPS rated lifespan: 12,000-24,000 hours (3-5 years effective operating life)
- LED rated lifespan: 80,000-100,000+ hours at L70 (15-20+ years effective life)

### Cost Comparison
- HPS lamp replacement cost: $300-500 AUD (lamp only)
- LED luminaire installed cost: $800-1,800 AUD (full luminaire with installation)
- HPS annual energy cost per light: approximately $140-180 AUD
- LED annual energy cost per light: approximately $50-70 AUD
- HPS maintenance: high cost, group lamp replacement every 3-4 years
- LED maintenance: low cost, negligible lamp replacement for 15+ years
- LED dimmability: excellent, 0-100% adaptive dimming profiles supported
- HPS dimmability: limited and poor quality

### Operating Assumptions for Melbourne
- Street lights operate approximately 4,200 hours per year (dusk to dawn averaged across seasons)
- Victorian unmetered public lighting electricity rate: $0.17-0.22 per kWh
- Electricity distributors: CitiPower (inner Melbourne), Powercor, Jemena, AusNet, United Energy

### Solar LED Street Lighting Suitability for Melbourne
Melbourne's latitude (37.8°S) creates a fundamental challenge for standalone solar street lighting:
- Winter (June): only 1.61 peak sun hours per day, but nights are approximately 14 hours long
- A 60W LED running 14 hours needs approximately 840 Wh per night
- A 300W solar panel at 1.61 peak sun hours with 80% system efficiency produces only 384 Wh per day — a significant energy deficit
- Standalone solar street lighting is therefore marginal for Melbourne and requires either oversized panels, large battery banks (3-5 days autonomy), or acceptance of dimmed winter operation

Solar LED is suitable for:
- Low-category paths (P5/P6/P10) requiring only 15-30W LEDs
- Locations far from the electricity grid where trenching costs are prohibitive
- Temporary or seasonal installations
- Hybrid solar/grid systems where solar supplements grid power

Grid-connected LED is the recommended primary energy-efficient solution for Melbourne street lighting.

## Victorian Carbon Emission Factors

Source: National Greenhouse Accounts Factors, Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water (DCCEEW)

- Scope 2 emission factor for Victoria: 0.96 kg CO2-equivalent per kWh
- Scope 3 emission factor for Victoria: 0.12 kg CO2-equivalent per kWh (upstream emissions)
- Total emission factor (Scope 2 + 3): 1.08 kg CO2-equivalent per kWh

Victoria has one of Australia's highest grid emission factors due to Latrobe Valley brown coal generation. The factor is declining approximately 3-5% per year as wind and solar capacity increases under the Victorian Renewable Energy Target (VRET) of 50% by 2030.

### Carbon Saving Example
For a single 150W HPS to 70W LED conversion using the combined Scope 2+3 factor of 1.08 kg CO2-e/kWh:
- Energy saving: 105W × 4,200 hours = 441 kWh per year
- Carbon saving: 441 × 1.08 = approximately 476 kg CO2-equivalent per year per light
- For a council with 10,000 street lights: approximately 4,760 tonnes CO2-equivalent per year

## Payback Period Benchmarks for LED Upgrades in Australia

### Typical Payback Calculations
- 150W HPS to 70W LED (minor road): capital $1,200 installed, annual saving $125 (energy + maintenance), payback 5-7 years without subsidy
- 250W HPS to 120W LED (major road): capital $1,500 installed, annual saving $170, payback 5-7 years without subsidy
- With Victorian Energy Efficiency Certificate (VEEC) subsidy: net capital reduced by $150-350 per light, payback 4-5 years
- With smart dimming controls: additional 20-30% energy saving on top of LED baseline, but adds $200-400 per light

### Victorian Energy Upgrades (VEU) Scheme
The VEU program provides tradeable Victorian Energy Efficiency Certificates (VEECs) for eligible energy efficiency activities including street light LED conversions.
- Certificates based on deemed 10-year energy saving
- VEEC market price: approximately $50-70 per certificate
- A 105W wattage reduction generates approximately 44 VEECs worth approximately $2,420

### Australian Council Case Studies
- Yarra Ranges Shire Council (VIC): upgraded 14,000+ lights, achieved 65% energy reduction, approximately 5 year payback
- City of Melbourne: major LED upgrade program as part of emissions reduction strategy
- Merri-bek (formerly Moreland) City Council: early adopter, achieved approximately 60% energy saving

## City of Melbourne Sustainability Context

- Net-zero emissions by 2040 target for council operations
- Street lighting typically accounts for 30-50% of a council's total electricity consumption
- Melbourne Renewable Energy Project (MREP): group power purchase agreement for 100% renewable electricity, effectively zeroing Scope 2 emissions
- Smart City lighting trials: adaptive dimming with sensors and IoT integration
- Urban Forest Strategy: warm colour temperature (3000K) to reduce ecological impact; shielded luminaires to reduce light spill into tree canopy
