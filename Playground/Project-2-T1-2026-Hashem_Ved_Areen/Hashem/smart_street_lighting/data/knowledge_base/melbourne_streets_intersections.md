# Melbourne Streets and Intersections — Lighting Context

Source: Compiled from City of Melbourne data, VicRoads guidelines, and pedestrian safety reports.

## Major Melbourne Streets

### Swanston Street
- Classification: Major urban arterial through CBD
- V-category: V2 (1.5 cd/m2 average luminance)
- Pedestrian activity: Extremely high (1,500-3,900 pedestrians/hour peak)
- Key intersections: Flinders St, Collins St, Bourke St, La Trobe St
- Special: Tram corridor — luminaires must not cause glare to tram drivers
- Current lighting: Mix of heritage and modern LED luminaires

### Collins Street
- Classification: Major urban arterial
- V-category: V2-V3 depending on section
- Pedestrian activity: Very high (1,000-2,300 pedestrians/hour in CBD section)
- Notable: Theatre district (eastern end) requires enhanced evening lighting
- Paris End (eastern): Heritage lighting design requirements

### Bourke Street
- Classification: Urban arterial with pedestrian mall section
- Bourke Street Mall: P1 category (14 lux minimum), pedestrian-only
- Remainder: V3 category
- Pedestrian activity: Very high in mall section

### Flinders Street
- Classification: Major arterial adjacent to Flinders Street Station
- V-category: V2
- Pedestrian activity: Extremely high at station end (busiest pedestrian area in Melbourne)
- Special: Station forecourt lighting integrated with heritage architecture

### St Kilda Road
- Classification: Boulevard arterial
- V-category: V1-V2
- Special: Tree-lined boulevard — luminaire placement must avoid canopy conflict
- Dual carriageway with central median: central median arrangement preferred

## Intersection Lighting Requirements

### Signalised Intersections
Per AS/NZS 1158.4 and VicRoads guidelines:
- Light the intersection to one V-category step above the highest road entering
- Example: V3 road meets V4 road → intersection lit to V2 standard
- Minimum area: 20m before stop line on each approach
- Pedestrian crossing areas within intersection: 50 lux vertical illuminance

### Roundabouts
- Central island and circulating carriageway: lit to V-category of highest approach road
- Approach roads: normal V-category for 50m before roundabout
- Pedestrian crossings at roundabout entries: AS/NZS 1158.4 requirements
- Splitter island pedestrian refuges: minimum P3 lighting (7 lux)

### School Zone Crossings
- Same illuminance requirements as AS/NZS 1158.4 standard crossings
- Additional VicRoads requirements: flashing 40km/h signs, emu crossing or school crossing supervisor
- Operating hours: typically 30 minutes before and after school times
- Lighting must be adequate during winter afternoon dismissal (4:00-4:30 PM, already dusk)
- Recommended: smart controls to boost lighting during school zone active hours

### Mid-Block Pedestrian Crossings
- 50 lux vertical illuminance on pedestrian at 1.5m height
- Horizontal illuminance across crossing: double the adjacent road section
- Approach lighting: enhanced for 20m on driver approach side
- Asymmetric luminaires or supplementary poles commonly used

## High-Pedestrian Intersections in Melbourne

### Flinders Street / Swanston Street
- Busiest intersection in Melbourne by pedestrian volume
- Estimated 3,000-4,000 pedestrians per hour during peak
- Current lighting: V1 equivalent with supplementary pedestrian lighting
- CCTV integration requires minimum 4 lux vertical illuminance

### Flinders Street / Elizabeth Street
- Major tram interchange with high pedestrian crossing volumes
- Complex intersection geometry with multiple crossing points
- Lighting must cover all pedestrian desire lines, not just formal crossings

### Collins Street / Spencer Street (Southern Cross)
- Station precinct with very high evening pedestrian activity
- Covered concourse transitions to open street — lighting must manage adaptation
- P1 category minimum for station approach areas

### Swanston Street / La Trobe Street
- Melbourne Central shopping centre and State Library entrance
- Very high pedestrian volumes throughout the day
- Heritage precinct lighting requirements near State Library

## Safety Data Context

### Pedestrian Crash Statistics (Melbourne CBD)
- Approximately 200 pedestrian crashes per year in City of Melbourne
- 60% occur at intersections
- 40% occur during darkness or twilight conditions
- Highest risk: 5pm-7pm in winter (dark, peak pedestrian activity)
- Key risk factors: inadequate crossing illuminance, driver sight distance, pedestrian visibility

### Lighting and Safety Correlation
- Studies show adequate street lighting reduces nighttime pedestrian crashes by 50-65%
- The most effective intervention: improving vertical illuminance at crossings (pedestrian visibility to drivers)
- Upgrading from V5 to V3 at an intersection typically reduces nighttime crashes by 30%
- LED lighting (better colour rendering, CRI 70+) improves pedestrian detection compared to HPS (CRI 25)

## Cost Estimates for Intersection Upgrades

### Typical Intersection Lighting Upgrade
- Number of poles: 4-8 depending on intersection size
- LED luminaire per pole: 100-200W
- Installed cost per pole: $6,000-10,000 (includes luminaire, pole, wiring, trenching)
- Total intersection upgrade: $25,000-80,000
- Annual energy cost: $400-1,200
- Annual energy saving vs HPS: $600-1,800
- Retrofit payback: 8-15 years (new installation has no payback — it's new infrastructure)
