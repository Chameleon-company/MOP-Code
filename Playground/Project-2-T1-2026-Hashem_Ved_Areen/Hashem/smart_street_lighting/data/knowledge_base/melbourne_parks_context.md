# Melbourne Parks and Pathways — Urban Context for Lighting Design

Source: Compiled from City of Melbourne Open Data, parks information, and pedestrian activity data.

## Major Melbourne Parks and Gardens

### Fitzroy Gardens
- Location: East Melbourne, adjacent to CBD
- Area: approximately 26 hectares
- Key features: Heritage-listed garden, multiple pathways, Cooks' Cottage, Conservatory
- Pedestrian activity: High evening use, popular walking route between CBD and East Melbourne
- Pathway types: Wide shared-use paths (3-4m), heritage garden paths (1.5-2m)
- Current lighting: Mix of heritage-style and modern luminaires
- Recommended lighting category: P2 to P3 for main pathways, P9 for secondary garden paths
- Safety considerations: Well-used but some isolated sections near Treasury Gardens boundary

### Royal Park
- Location: Parkville, inner north
- Area: approximately 181 hectares (Melbourne's largest park)
- Key features: Native grasslands, wetlands, sporting facilities, Royal Melbourne Zoo adjacent
- Pedestrian activity: High on shared-use paths near zoo and sporting facilities; moderate to low on nature trails
- Pathway types: Major shared-use paths (3-4m), nature trails (1-2m)
- Recommended lighting category: P2 for major shared paths, P9-P10 for nature trails
- Safety considerations: Large area with isolated sections; ecological sensitivity (native wildlife)
- Special note: Ecological lighting design critical — 3000K warm white, shielded luminaires to protect nocturnal wildlife

### Princes Park
- Location: Carlton North
- Area: approximately 39 hectares
- Key features: Oval, running/cycling track, shared-use paths
- Pedestrian activity: Very high on perimeter shared path (popular running route); moderate internal paths
- Pathway types: Perimeter shared path (3-4m), internal park paths
- Recommended lighting category: P2 for perimeter path, P3 for internal paths

### Carlton Gardens
- Location: Carlton, adjacent to CBD
- Area: approximately 26 hectares
- Key features: UNESCO World Heritage listed (Royal Exhibition Building), formal garden paths, mature tree canopy
- Pedestrian activity: High — transit route between CBD and Carlton
- Recommended lighting category: P2 to P3 for main paths
- Special note: Heritage lighting design requirements; lighting must complement heritage landscape

### Birrarung Marr
- Location: Between CBD and Yarra River
- Area: approximately 8 hectares
- Key features: Event space, public art, terraced pathways along river
- Pedestrian activity: Very high during events, moderate otherwise
- Recommended lighting category: P1 to P2 during events, P3 baseline
- Special note: Event lighting infrastructure important for temporary installations

### Flagstaff Gardens
- Location: West Melbourne, adjacent to CBD
- Area: approximately 7 hectares
- Key features: Oldest park in Melbourne, playground, lawn areas, mature trees
- Pedestrian activity: Moderate to high during day, lower in evening
- Recommended lighting category: P3 for main paths, P5 for secondary paths

## Melbourne Pathway Design Considerations

### Shared-Use Paths
- Width: typically 3.0-4.0 metres
- Users: pedestrians, cyclists, runners
- Minimum lighting: P3 (7 lux average) for high-use paths per City of Melbourne standards
- Pole height: 5-6 metres (higher to illuminate wider path)
- Pole spacing: 20-30 metres depending on luminaire output

### Pedestrian-Only Park Paths
- Width: typically 1.5-2.5 metres
- Users: pedestrians, dog walkers
- Minimum lighting: P9 (2 lux average) for moderate use, P10 (1 lux) for low use
- Pole height: 3.5-5.0 metres
- Pole spacing: 15-25 metres for P9, 20-35 metres for P10
- Bollard lighting may be appropriate for heritage or garden settings

### Park Entrances and Gathering Areas
- Lighting category: P1 (14 lux) or P2 (10 lux)
- Vertical illuminance required for facial recognition and CCTV
- Wider illumination area to cover seating, signage, and approach paths

## Pedestrian Activity Patterns in Melbourne Parks

### Seasonal Variation
- Summer (Dec-Feb): Peak evening activity from 6pm-10pm; sunset approximately 8:30-9:00pm
- Autumn (Mar-May): Evening activity 5pm-8pm; sunset approximately 5:30-7:30pm
- Winter (Jun-Aug): Limited evening activity; sunset approximately 5:00-5:30pm; lighting critical for safety of remaining users
- Spring (Sep-Nov): Growing evening activity; sunset approximately 6:00-8:00pm

### Weekly Patterns
- Weekday evenings: Commuter foot traffic (5pm-7pm), recreational use (6pm-9pm)
- Weekend evenings: Higher and more sustained recreational use
- Event nights: Significant spikes at Birrarung Marr, Flagstaff Gardens, Carlton Gardens

### High-Activity Periods Requiring Full Lighting
- Weekday: 5:00pm to 11:00pm
- Weekend: 5:00pm to midnight
- Events: Duration of event plus 1 hour dispersal time

### Low-Activity Periods (Suitable for Dimming)
- Midnight to 5:00am on most nights
- Dimming to P5 or lower acceptable if path remains navigable and safe
