# CPTED Principles for Street Lighting Design

## Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design (CPTED)

CPTED is a multi-disciplinary approach to deterring criminal behaviour through environmental design. Lighting is one of the most critical CPTED strategies for public spaces.

## Core CPTED Lighting Principles

### 1. Natural Surveillance
- Lighting must enable people to see and be seen from a distance of at least 15 metres
- Faces should be recognisable at 10 metres (requires minimum 4 lux at face height)
- Avoid creating pools of light separated by dark areas — uniform coverage is critical
- AS/NZS 1158 uniformity ratios (Emin/Eavg ≥ 0.50 for pedestrian areas) directly support this

### 2. Sightlines and Visibility
- Entry/exit points of parks and pathways must be well-lit (minimum P3 category)
- Curves and bends in pathways require additional lighting to maintain sightlines
- Intersections where multiple paths meet are high-priority locations for lighting
- Vegetation should not obstruct lighting — maintenance schedules should address this

### 3. Concealment Reduction
- Lighting should eliminate deep shadows near structures, dense plantings, and recesses
- Bollard lighting alone is insufficient — pole-mounted luminaires at 4-6m height provide broader coverage
- Asymmetric luminaire distributions can direct light into areas of concern

### 4. Activity Support
- Higher P-categories (P1-P3) in areas with evening/night activities encourage legitimate use
- Well-lit spaces attract more pedestrians, creating a natural surveillance effect
- Event spaces and gathering areas benefit from flexible lighting levels

## CPTED-Informed P-Category Adjustments

When crime data indicates elevated risk, the following adjustments are recommended:

| Baseline Category | Risk Level | Adjusted Category | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| P9 (park path, moderate) | Moderate crime | P8 or P7 | Improve visibility for safety |
| P9 (park path, moderate) | High crime | P5 or P4 | Ensure face recognition distance |
| P10 (park path, low use) | Moderate crime | P9 | Maintain minimum safe level |
| P10 (park path, low use) | High crime | P7 | Discourage criminal activity |
| P3 (moderate pedestrian) | High crime | P2 or P1 | Maximum visibility in risk areas |

## Victorian Context

- Victoria Police recommends CPTED assessments for all new public lighting projects
- City of Melbourne's Public Lighting Strategy references CPTED principles
- Parks Victoria requires CPTED-compliant lighting at all trailheads and car parks
- The Victorian Planning Authority mandates CPTED considerations in precinct structure plans

## Colour Temperature Considerations

- Standard recommendation: 3000K (warm white) for ecological reasons
- CPTED consideration: 4000K (neutral white) improves colour rendering and facial recognition
- Compromise: 3000K with CRI ≥ 70 maintains safety while reducing ecological impact
- Blue-rich light (>5000K) is NOT recommended due to wildlife impact and glare
