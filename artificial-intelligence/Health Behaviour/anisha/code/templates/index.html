What is Food Security?

Food security ensures that all people have access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food for a healthy life. It encompasses:

  • Availability: Consistent supply of food.
  • Access: Ability to afford and obtain food.
  • Utilization: Proper use and absorption of nutrients.
  • Stability: Reliable access to food over time.

Why Does It Matter?

Food security is vital for the health and stability of communities. It impacts:

  • Health: Reduces malnutrition and improves cognitive and physical development.
  • Economic Stability: Promotes productivity and reduces healthcare costs.
  • Education: Enhances school attendance and performance through better nutrition.
  • Social Stability: Prevents unrest and supports community cohesion.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Encourages sustainable agricultural practices.

Food Insecurity and the COVID-19 Pandemic

  • The COVID-19 pandemic led to widespread job losses, reducing access to affordable and nutritious food for many families.
  • Supply chain disruptions caused food shortages and price hikes, exacerbating the challenges faced by vulnerable communities.
  • Low-income families and individuals were disproportionately affected, with a significant reliance on food relief programs in both urban and rural areas.
  • Melbourne saw a surge in demand for food aid, highlighting the critical role of local organizations and community efforts.

As recovery continues, rebuilding resilient food systems is essential to ensuring equitable access to food in future crises. Strengthening local food networks and enhancing community resilience are key priorities for the City of Melbourne.

Planning for a Food Secure City Of Melbourne (2021-2025)

While food insecurity is not new, the COVID-19 pandemic heightened the need for better food access and increased social housing investment. This plan focuses on:

  • Supporting local food relief organizations.
  • Developing sustainable and equitable food systems.
  • Enhancing community gardens and urban farming initiatives.
  • Fostering collaboration between government and NGOs.

The City of Melbourne aims to create a resilient food system that ensures everyone has access to affordable and nutritious food.
