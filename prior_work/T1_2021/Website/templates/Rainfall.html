Exploratory Data Analysis on Rainfall

Below are various graphs to demonstrate the trend of the weather variable ‘Rainfall (mm)’. The daily rainfall forecasts. are probably the most viewed part of our city forecast. The rainfall dataset was collected from Australian Government Bureau of Meteorology (BOM), which is range from January 2015 to March 2021. This information is useful for people in Melbourne who are planning outdoor activities, or whose work or travel may be dependent upon the weather. As a result, we believe that our pedestrian system output is associated with rainfall feature.

The figure below is scatter plot to view overall distribution of rainfall. It gives us insight the relationship between rainfall and time series (January 2015 to March 2021). In details, most days are sunny because we can see a large number of points clustered at 0mm. Furthermore, there are extremely mist day especially in 2020.The max rainfall amount is 54.6mm happened on 2020-03-05. And the average rainfall amount in 2020 is higher than average rainfall amount in other years

This histogram displays the distribution of rainfall amount from January 2015 to March across Melbourne. The average and median costs are 1.5mm and 0 mm, respectively. Given that the rainfall data is unimodal, with one local maximum and a right skew, the fact that the average is greater than the median, matches our expectation. The range of rainfall amount is very large, 55mm with the smallest rainfall amount equal to 0mm and the largest rainfall amount equal to 54.6mm. There is one day, 2020-05-03, that rainfall amount far more than the rest at 54.6mm.

This Bar chart display the distribution of maximum rainfall amount group by year. The largest rainfall happened in 2020, with 54.6mm whereas 2019 has smallest rainfall, at 15.4mm. 2015, 2017, 2018, 2021 all has maximum or 30-40 mm or less, while 2016 has maximum rainfall of about 22mm.

This bar chart shows maximum rainfall amount grouping by the day of week. From the figure above, we can see that maximum rainfall amount on Thursday is highest, with 54.6mm whereas the Friday has smallest maximum rainfall amount, at 25.2mm. Furthermore, the Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday has similar maximum rainfall amount, with 35mm or more and less. There is no obvious pattern relating to day of week, in terms of maximum rainfall amount across Melbourne.

This bar chart shows maximum rainfall amount grouping by month. From Dec to April, maximum rainfall amount happened on these five months are higher than maximum rainfall from May to Nov. The Mar has largest maximum rainfall amount, with 54.6mm. whereas the Jun has smallest maximum rainfall amount, at 15.6mm. Interestingly, the maximum rainfall amount increases from 21.4 to 44mm, relating months from Sep to Jan. As a result, we may infer that, in period from Sep to Mar, there may extremely weather condition happen.

When looking at the histogram it is clear that our observations from the previous two graphs are substantiated. With a skew of 0.23 this shows more outliers on the maximum end than on the minimum end.
